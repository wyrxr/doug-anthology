[An exploration of both the "bit" as Twitch currency and as a mode of mediated performance—a "magic circle" that streamer and audience enter and actively participate in, transforming acts that would be absurd in the "ordinary" world into soft contractual obligations (playing by the "rules of the game")]

The Bit justifies frivolity. America is a society governed by the spectral logic of its "Protestant work ethic," where all of life must be organized around and conducise to pious living. Capitalism, having replaced God in the minds and hearts of most Americans, similarly demands a singular devotion to "productivity." The Bit provides that brief but necessary respite from industrialist efficiency.

The "thick" vs "thin" world—rational, categorical, cut to size vs the irrational, reconciliation of opposites, and acceptance of multiplicity.

Dr. Dob Goblana: "The signifier may not align with anything we can taste, touch, smell, feel, or see, but it is not empty. It is filled with shadows, half-misunderstood meanings, and memories of the future."

Discourse is undermined in the postmodern condition by an awareness that every sincere expression must be mediated by performance and construction. We are aware of how thoroughly problematized the Grand Narrative is, yet we yearn for the purity and simplicity that these worldviews provide.

The postmodern mind is fractured and must subsist in personas.

DougDoug collapses the "vexatious distance between art and audience" (Dada IS Bathos!, 31). The livestream mirrors the "beauty of the work's evanescence" (ibid).
